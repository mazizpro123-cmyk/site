Common Brushing
Mistakes

These everyday habits silently damage your teeth. Learn what to stop doing — and why it matters.

OOPS
The Top 5 mistakes people make:

01
💪

Brushing Too Hard

Pressing too hard wears away enamel — your teeth's protective outer layer — and causes gum recession that can't grow back. Once exposed, roots become sensitive to hot, cold, and sweet.

What happens: Enamel erosion & gum recession
Fix: Use light pressure — bristles shouldn't flatten
02
🌙

Skipping Night Brushing

During sleep, saliva production drops dramatically. Bacteria left on teeth feast on food particles for hours. This is when cavities and plaque form fastest. Night brushing is non-negotiable.

What happens: Rapid plaque buildup, cavity formation
Fix: Always brush before bed, no exceptions
03
🪥

Using an Old Toothbrush

Frayed and worn bristles lose their ability to remove plaque effectively. Old brushes also harbor bacteria in damaged bristles. The American Dental Association recommends replacing every 3 months.

What happens: Reduced cleaning efficiency, bacteria buildup
Fix: Replace brush every 3 months — mark calendar
04
🧵

Skipping Flossing

Your toothbrush only cleans about 60% of tooth surfaces. The spaces between teeth — where floss reaches — are prime spots for plaque, tartar, and eventual gum disease to develop undetected.

What happens: Plaque between teeth, gingivitis, bad breath
Fix: Floss at least once daily before bed
05
🍬

Excess Sugar Intake

Sugar feeds the bacteria in your mouth which produce acid as a byproduct. This acid attacks and erodes enamel. It's not just candy — fruit juice, sports drinks, and crackers are just as damaging.

What happens: Acid attacks, enamel erosion, tooth decay
Fix: Limit sugar, drink water after sugary foods
