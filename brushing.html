The Method

Step-by-step
brushing guide

📐
01

45° Angle

Hold the toothbrush at a 45-degree angle toward the gumline. This position lets bristles clean both the tooth surface and the edge of the gums simultaneously.

Pro tip Point bristles toward where the tooth meets the gum.
⭕
02

Gentle Circular Motion

Use small, gentle circular strokes — never scrub back and forth. Circular motion lifts plaque without damaging enamel or causing gum recession.

Pro tip Think "massage" not "scrub". Light pressure works better.
🗺️
03

Cover All Surfaces

Brush outer, inner, and chewing surfaces of every tooth. Most people miss the inner surfaces of lower front teeth — take your time.

Pro tip Divide mouth into 4 zones — 30 seconds each.
👅
04

Clean Your Tongue

Gently brush or scrape your tongue after teeth. The tongue harbors bacteria that cause bad breath and can recontaminate your freshly cleaned teeth.

Pro tip Use a dedicated tongue scraper for best results.
🦷
05

Right Toothpaste

Use a fluoride toothpaste (1000–1500 ppm for adults). A pea-sized amount is enough. Fluoride strengthens enamel and prevents cavities effectively.

Pro tip Don't rinse with water after — let fluoride work.
⏰
06

Right Timing

Brush twice daily — once in the morning and once before bed. The night brush is most critical as saliva flow decreases during sleep.

Pro tip Wait 30 min after eating acidic foods before brushing.
